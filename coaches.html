Our Coaches

Led by Grand Master German López Alvarez, our coaching staff is dedicated to teaching discipline, leadership, confidence, and martial arts excellence.

Grand Master López began practicing Taekwondo in 1975 in Tehuacán, Puebla, Mexico and continued his martial arts journey after moving to Los Angeles, California in 1981.

In 1984, he founded the Azteca Taekwondo Federation in Los Angeles and has since organized international championships between the United States and Mexico while mentoring generations of martial artists.

He has taught at East Los Angeles College, worked with the Los Angeles County Sheriff Department, participated in Kukkiwon leadership forums in Korea, and represented the Taekwondo community internationally.

In 2024, Grand Master López earned his 9th Degree Black Belt, the highest rank in Taekwondo, representing decades of dedication, leadership, and excellence in martial arts.

Master Mitzi Barroso and Instructor Hector Soriano lead our competition sparring program, helping students grow both as athletes and individuals through discipline, determination, teamwork, and competitive experience.

Their coaching focuses on building confidence, strong technique, mental toughness, sportsmanship, and inspiring students to reach their highest potential in Taekwondo and in everyday life.
